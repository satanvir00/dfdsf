Novak Djokovic made the\u00a0Wimbledon\u00a0fourth round for the 15th time on Friday, beating old rival Stan Wawrinka and a night-time curfew as Andy Murray hinted his All England Club days may be over.<\/p>\n

Djokovic, chasing a record-equalling eighth\u00a0Wimbledon\u00a0title and 24th career Grand Slam crown, came through 6-3, 6-1, 7-6 (7\/5) against the 38-year-old Wawrinka.<\/p>\n

World number two Djokovic will face Poland\u2019s Hubert Hurkacz, who he has defeated five times in as many meetings, for a place in the quarter-finals.<\/p>\n

Friday\u2019s win was Djokovic\u2019s 21st in 27 meetings with Wawrinka, a three-time major champion who famously defeated the Serb in the 2015 French Open and 2016 US Open finals.<\/p>\n

Djokovic never faced a break point under the Centre Court roof and completed victory just 15 minutes before the 11pm All England Club curfew kicked in otherwise they would have had to return on Saturday.<\/p>\n

\u201cStan\u2019s doing an amazing job for his age \u2014 we are two old guys,\u201d said 36-year-old Djokovic who went level with Pete Sampras\u2019s mark of 31 successive wins at the tournament.<\/p>\n

\u201cWe\u2019ve had great battles over the years. I respect him as a player and love him as a person. He\u2019s a really nice guy.\u201d<\/p>\n

Djokovic\u2019s win kept him on course for a championship match showdown with world number one and US Open champion Carlos Alcaraz who defeated 84th-ranked Alexandre Muller of France 6-4, 7-6 (7\/2), 6-3.<\/p>\n

The 20-year-old Alcaraz will face Chile\u2019s Nicolas Jarry for a spot in the last 16.<\/p>\n

Murray admitted he doesn\u2019t know if he will be back at\u00a0Wimbledon\u00a0after going down 7-6 (7\/3), 6-7 (2\/7), 4-6, 7-6 (7\/3), 6-4 to fifth-ranked Stefanos Tsitsipas in a four-hour 40-minute second round epic.<\/p>\n

The 36-year-old has not made the second week of a Grand Slam since reaching the quarter-finals at\u00a0Wimbledon\u00a0in 2017.<\/p>\n

\u201cI don\u2019t know,\u201d said former world number one Murray when asked if he would be back in 2024.<\/p>\n

\u201cMotivation is obviously a big thing. Continuing having early losses in tournaments like this don\u2019t necessarily help with that.\u201d<\/p>\n

Tsitispas fired 90 winners past Murray and goes on to face Laslo Djere of Serbia for a place in the last 16.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s never easy against Andy. Everyone loves him here,\u201d said the 24-year-old Greek.<\/p>\n

The mercurial Russian was two sets and 4-4 ahead when play was halted on day four but quickly wrapped up victory in a third set tie-break.<\/p>\n

Danish sixth seed Holger Rune reached the third round for the first time, brushing aside Spain\u2019s Roberto Carballes Baena 6-3, 7-6 (7\/3), 6-4.<\/p>\n

Seventh seeded Andrey Rublev of Russia and Italian eighth-seed Jannik Sinner made the last 16 with wins over David Goffin and Quentin Halys respectively.<\/p>\n
